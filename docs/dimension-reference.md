# Sensory Dimension Quick Reference

This document is a compact lookup reference for the 113 sensory dimensions used in the ZeroScraps sensory compound system. Each entry captures the perceptual mechanism, scale characteristics, anchor ingredients, natural-language tertiary descriptors, common co-occurrences with other dimensions, and dose behavior. It complements the main culinary-identity and sensory-compound knowledge bases.

**Scale conventions**
- Unipolar dims: 0 (absent) through 6 (dominant / saturating).
- Bipolar dim (Temperature only): -6 (frozen) through +6 (very hot).
- **Stevens exponent** describes how perceived intensity scales with physical concentration (from Stevens' power law `S = kI^n`). Values < 1 mean the sense saturates quickly (small amounts feel nearly as strong as large); values > 1 mean perceived intensity grows faster than concentration.

**Dose terminology**
- **Trace** — detectable, supports but does not define profile.
- **Moderate** — contributes identifiable character, typically 1-3 on scale.
- **Dominant** — defines the dish's perceptual identity, typically 4-6.

## TASTE (7 dimensions)

### 0. Sweet
**Category**: taste | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: T1R2/T1R3 heterodimer receptors on the tongue bind mono/disaccharides and some high-intensity sweeteners.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0 (near-linear)
**Anchor examples**: sucrose, honey, ripe mango, date paste, maple syrup
**Tertiary descriptors**: sugary, honeyed, candied, jammy, syrupy, saccharine, confectionary, sucrose-forward, dessert-like, frosted, glazed, sweetened, cloying, nectared
**Co-occurs with**: Sour (balance), Floral, Fruity aromas, Warm Sweet Spice
**Dose behavior**: trace below 2% mass sucrose equivalent; moderate 5-10%; dominant above 15% — beyond ~25% becomes cloying and suppresses other tastes.

### 1. Salty
**Category**: taste | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Na+ passes ENaC channels; secondary contribution from TRPV1 at high concentrations.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.3 (supralinear — salt rises sharply with concentration)
**Anchor examples**: table salt, soy sauce, anchovy, fish sauce, parmesan
**Tertiary descriptors**: salty, saline, briny, savory-salt, sodium-forward, seasoned, cured-salt, pickle-brine, mineral-salty, mouth-puckering-salt
**Co-occurs with**: Umami, Briny, Fermented, Cured-Preserved
**Dose behavior**: trace 0.1-0.5% NaCl by mass; optimal 0.8-1.2% for most savory dishes; dominant above 1.5% — above 2% most palates reject.

### 2. Sour
**Category**: taste | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: H+ ions block K+ channels on taste cells and activate PKD2L1/OTOP1 proton channels.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: lemon juice, vinegar, sour cream, tamarind, green apple
**Tertiary descriptors**: sour, tart, tangy, acidic, puckering, sharp-acid, zingy, zesty, citric, vinegary, acerbic, bright-acid, mouth-watering
**Co-occurs with**: Sweet, Citrusy, Acetic-Vinegar, Lacto-Pickled, Fresh
**Dose behavior**: trace at pH ~4.5; moderate pH 3.5-4.0; dominant pH below 3.2 — extreme acidity triggers aversion and damages enamel.

### 3. Bitter
**Category**: taste | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: T2R family (~25 receptors) detects diverse alkaloids, glucosinolates, polyphenols.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.7 (sublinear — saturates quickly, innately aversive)
**Anchor examples**: espresso, dark chocolate, radicchio, IPA hops, bitter melon, rapini
**Tertiary descriptors**: bitter, astringent-bitter, acrid, hoppy, earthy-bitter, chalky-bitter, medicinal, tannic-bitter, herbaceous-bitter, chicory-like, quinine
**Co-occurs with**: Astringent, Coffee-Roast, Cocoa-Roast, Brassica, Dark Cocoa
**Dose behavior**: detection threshold extremely low (e.g., quinine at 8 µM); moderate at 1-3 intensity adds complexity; dominant above 4 is usually rejected unless paired with sweetness or fat.

### 4. Umami
**Category**: taste | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: T1R1/T1R3 heterodimer + mGluR4 detects glutamate; synergistic with 5'-ribonucleotides (IMP, GMP).
**Stevens exponent**: 0.95
**Anchor examples**: parmesan, dried shiitake, tomato paste, MSG, anchovy, cured ham
**Tertiary descriptors**: savory, brothy, meaty-taste, mouth-filling, glutamate-rich, umami-deep, broth-taste, seaweed-savory, mushroomy-savory, satisfying
**Co-occurs with**: Salty, Kokumi, Broth-Stock, Aged Cheese, Cured-Preserved, Mushroom
**Dose behavior**: synergy amplifies ~8x when glutamate + IMP/GMP co-present; trace at 0.1% MSG equivalent; dominant at 0.5%+ — unlike salt/sugar, tolerance window is wide.

### 5. Kokumi
**Category**: taste | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Calcium-sensing receptor (CaSR) activation by gamma-glutamyl peptides (e.g., glutathione, GSH) — enhances mouthfulness, continuity, and thickness of other tastes.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.8
**Anchor examples**: aged gouda, long-cooked onions, garlic confit, scallop, fish sauce, bone broth
**Tertiary descriptors**: mouth-filling, lingering, rounded, enveloping, continuous, deep-savory, silky-savory, bloom, resonant, persistent, rich-mouth, full-bodied-taste
**Co-occurs with**: Umami, Fatty, Broth-Stock, Aged Cheese, Allium (cooked), Rich
**Dose behavior**: amplifies other tastes rather than standing alone; trace at 1-10 ppm glutathione; contributes most at dim 2-4 — rarely a sole dominant.

### 6. Fatty (Oleogustus)
**Category**: taste | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: CD36 and GPR120 receptors detect long-chain fatty acids (oleic, linoleic) independent of texture.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.75
**Anchor examples**: olive oil, butter, beef tallow, avocado, egg yolk, fatty tuna
**Tertiary descriptors**: fatty, oily-taste, lipid-rich, buttery-taste, marrow-like, lard-like, suet-like, tallowy, ghee-like, creamy-lipid, luxurious-fat
**Co-occurs with**: Creamy, Oily, Rich, Kokumi, Cooked Meat, Butter
**Dose behavior**: taste contribution distinct from mouthfeel; trace at <2% fat by mass; dominant at 15%+ — above ~30% begins to cloy and coat palate.

## CHEMESTHETIC (7 dimensions)

### 7. Spicy
**Category**: chemesthetic | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Capsaicin (and piperine) activate TRPV1 heat/pain receptors, producing a burning/heat sensation without thermal change.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.4 (strongly supralinear)
**Anchor examples**: chile de árbol, habanero, cayenne, Thai bird chile, gochugaru
**Tertiary descriptors**: hot, fiery, pepper-heat, burning, chile-heat, capsaicin-burn, searing, peppery-heat, scorching, blazing, tongue-tingling, lingering-burn
**Co-occurs with**: Warming, Numbing (ma la), Sweet (contrast), Savory Spice
**Dose behavior**: SHU scales nonlinearly with perception; trace at ~500 SHU dilution (cayenne dust); dominant above 30,000 SHU equivalent — builds on repeated exposure (sensitization).

### 8. Pungent
**Category**: chemesthetic | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Allyl isothiocyanate (mustard, wasabi, horseradish) and allicin (raw garlic, onion) activate TRPA1 — sharp, nasal, tearing sensation.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.3
**Anchor examples**: wasabi, horseradish, yellow mustard, raw onion, fresh garlic
**Tertiary descriptors**: sharp, sinus-piercing, nose-hit, stinging, eye-watering, mustardy-sharp, horseradish-bite, pungent-volatile, biting, lachrymal
**Co-occurs with**: Allium (raw), Brassica (raw), Warming
**Dose behavior**: volatile — dissipates in seconds after application; trace below 0.1% AITC; dominant at 1%+ — cooking largely destroys pungency.

### 9. Cooling
**Category**: chemesthetic | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Menthol, eucalyptol, and related terpenes activate TRPM8 cold receptors, mimicking low temperatures.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0
**Anchor examples**: peppermint, spearmint, eucalyptus, wintergreen, menthol
**Tertiary descriptors**: cooling, minty, refreshing, icy-mouth, fresh-cool, menthol-cool, breeze-like, glacial, crisp-cool, tingly-cool
**Co-occurs with**: Fresh, Floral-Herbal, Fresh Herb, Sweet
**Dose behavior**: threshold ~0.5 ppm menthol; moderate 5-20 ppm; dominant above 50 ppm — can induce anesthesia at extremes.

### 10. Astringent
**Category**: chemesthetic | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Polyphenols (tannins, catechins) precipitate salivary proteins, producing a puckering/drying sensation.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: unripe persimmon, strong black tea, red wine, pomegranate, walnut skin
**Tertiary descriptors**: puckering, drying, tannic, mouth-gripping, chalky-astringent, rough, cottony, fur-like, constricting, tea-dry
**Co-occurs with**: Bitter, Barrel-Aged, Dried Fruit, Tree Nut (skin-on)
**Dose behavior**: trace 0.1 g/L tannin equivalent; balanced 0.5-1.5 g/L; dominant above 2.5 g/L — sensation persists and cumulates across sips.

### 11. Numbing
**Category**: chemesthetic | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Hydroxy-alpha-sanshool (Sichuan/Szechuan pepper) activates RET and KCNK channels at ~50 Hz, producing a buzzing paresthesia.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.1
**Anchor examples**: Sichuan peppercorn, Japanese sansho, Tasmanian pepperberry, grains of paradise
**Tertiary descriptors**: numbing, tingling, buzzing, vibrating, electric, paresthetic, lip-tingle, ma-la, effervescent-mouth, anesthetic-tingle
**Co-occurs with**: Spicy (ma la), Warming, Pepper, Citrusy
**Dose behavior**: trace 0.1% sanshool by mass; dominant at 0.5%+ — effect is freshness-dependent and fades within minutes of grinding.

### 12. Carbonated
**Category**: chemesthetic | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: CO2 converts to carbonic acid on mucosal surfaces, activating carbonic anhydrase IV and TRPA1 — producing prickly, acidic, effervescent tingle.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.2
**Anchor examples**: champagne, seltzer, kombucha, pop rocks, carbonated fruit
**Tertiary descriptors**: fizzy, bubbly, effervescent, sparkling, prickly-bubble, tingly-CO2, crackling, spritzy, champagne-like, bead-lively
**Co-occurs with**: Sour, Sweet, Fruity aromas, Fresh
**Dose behavior**: trace 1-2 g/L dissolved CO2; optimal 4-5 g/L (beer/soft drinks); dominant 7-12 g/L (champagne) — perception is pressure-sensitive.

### 13. Warming
**Category**: chemesthetic | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Ethanol and gingerols (6-shogaol when dried/cooked) activate TRPV1 at lower threshold than capsaicin, producing diffuse warmth without sharp burn.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.1
**Anchor examples**: aged whiskey, fresh ginger, cinnamon bark oil, grains of paradise, long pepper
**Tertiary descriptors**: warming, glowing, gentle-heat, radiant-warm, ethanol-warm, ginger-warm, spice-warm, diffuse-heat, hearth-like, blooming-warm
**Co-occurs with**: Spicy (lower intensity), Barrel-Aged, Warm Sweet Spice, Numbing
**Dose behavior**: ethanol threshold ~8% ABV for warmth; ginger gingerol threshold ~0.5 mg/g; dominant at 2+ mg/g shogaol — perceived lower-throat rather than tongue.

## AROMA — FRUITY FAMILY (7 secondary dimensions)

### 14. Citrusy
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fruity | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Limonene, citral, linalool, and citrus-specific sesquiterpenes from peel oil sacs and juice vesicles.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: lemon zest, lime, grapefruit, yuzu, bergamot, orange peel
**Tertiary descriptors**: citrusy, lemony, zesty, limey, grapefruity, bergamot-like, orange-peel, yuzu-like, citrus-oil, bright-peel, zested, fresh-citrus, candied-peel
**Co-occurs with**: Sour, Fresh, Blossom-Citrus, Floral-Delicate, Cooling
**Dose behavior**: trace at peel scrape/zest; moderate with juice/zest combo; dominant in confit or cello — top note evaporates fast (limonene t1/2 ~30 min at 60°C).

### 15. Tropical
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fruity | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Sulfur-containing thiols (4-MMP in passionfruit), lactones (gamma-decalactone in peach/mango), and esters (ethyl butyrate).
**Stevens exponent**: 0.8
**Anchor examples**: ripe mango, passionfruit, pineapple, guava, papaya, lychee
**Tertiary descriptors**: tropical, mangoey, passionfruit-like, pineappley, guava-like, lychee-floral, jackfruit, papaya-soft, thiol-pungent-tropical, sunny-fruit
**Co-occurs with**: Sweet, Floral-Delicate, Sour, Fresh, Tree Nut (coconut-adjacent)
**Dose behavior**: highly concentration-dependent — thiols pleasant at ppb level, rancid at ppm; dominant when pulp ≥20% of dish mass.

### 16. Berry
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fruity | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Anthocyanin-derived precursors; raspberry ketone, furaneol in strawberry, methyl anthranilate in grape/blueberry.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: strawberry, raspberry, blueberry, blackberry, black currant
**Tertiary descriptors**: berry-like, jammy-berry, bramble, raspberry-ketone, strawberry-candied, cassis, currant, blueberry-muffin, wild-berry, compote
**Co-occurs with**: Sweet, Sour, Floral-Delicate, Vanilla (pairing), Dried Fruit
**Dose behavior**: trace with garnish; moderate with coulis; dominant when reduced into sauce — oxidation darkens aroma from bright to jammy within hours of crushing.

### 17. Orchard Stone
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fruity | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Gamma- and delta-lactones (C10-C12), benzaldehyde from pit kernels, and specific C6-C9 esters.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.8
**Anchor examples**: ripe peach, apricot, plum, sour cherry, nectarine, mirabelle
**Tertiary descriptors**: stone-fruit, peachy, apricot-like, plummy, cherry-pit, almond-pit (benzaldehyde), nectarine, drupe-like, lactonic-soft, velvet-fruit
**Co-occurs with**: Sweet, Floral-Delicate, Tree Nut, Vanilla, Barrel-Aged
**Dose behavior**: trace from garnish slice; moderate at ~10% pulp; dominant in reductions — lactones amplify with gentle cooking and diminish with burn.

### 18. Orchard Pomme
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fruity | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Hexyl acetate, 2-methylbutyl acetate, farnesene — the "apple" ester/sesquiterpene family shared by pome fruits.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.8
**Anchor examples**: Honeycrisp apple, Bartlett pear, Asian pear, quince, medlar
**Tertiary descriptors**: apple-like, pear-like, quince-like, pomme, cider-fresh, crisp-apple, russet, bruised-apple, waxy-peel, orchard-fresh, baked-apple (when cooked)
**Co-occurs with**: Sweet, Sour, Floral-Delicate, Warm Sweet Spice (baked forms), Barrel-Aged (cider)
**Dose behavior**: volatile — fresh-cut peak dissipates within hours; moderate when juiced; dominant in reductions or ciders; cooked forms shift toward Toffee-Caramel.

### 19. Melon
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fruity | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: 2,6-nonadienal (cucumber/melon aldehyde), ethyl methylpropanoate, and cis-3-hexenol give the characteristic watery, green-sweet aroma.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.75
**Anchor examples**: cantaloupe, honeydew, watermelon, Galia melon, casaba
**Tertiary descriptors**: melony, cantaloupe-like, honeydew-soft, watermelon-cool, cucumber-adjacent, green-melon, watery-fruit, refreshing-melon, summer-fruit, vine-fresh
**Co-occurs with**: Fresh, Grassy, Juicy, Sweet (low), Floral-Delicate
**Dose behavior**: extremely delicate aroma — trace at fresh slice; moderate in juice; rarely dominant — heat destroys the diagnostic aldehydes within minutes.

### 20. Dried Fruit
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fruity | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Concentration of sugars + Maillard/caramelization during drying; formation of hydroxymethylfurfural and long-chain esters; loss of volatile top notes.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: Medjool dates, dried fig, raisin, prune, dried apricot, dried cherry
**Tertiary descriptors**: dried-fruit, raisin-like, prune-like, date-deep, fig-seedy, sun-dried, concentrated-fruit, candied-dried, leathery-sweet, stewed-fruit
**Co-occurs with**: Sweet, Molasses-Dark, Warm Sweet Spice, Barrel-Aged, Rich
**Dose behavior**: trace as garnish; moderate when chopped into dish; dominant in tagines, fruitcakes, and pastes — flavor depth positively correlates with drying temperature up to ~65°C.

## AROMA — FLORAL FAMILY (4 dimensions)

### 21. Floral-Delicate
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Floral | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Phenylethanol (rose), benzyl acetate (jasmine), linalool and low-level indole — delicate rosaceous/white-floral volatiles.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.7
**Anchor examples**: rose petal, jasmine tea, elderflower, violet, osmanthus
**Tertiary descriptors**: floral, rosy, jasmine-like, elderflower, violet-sweet, petal-like, perfumed, powdery-floral, white-floral, romantic, delicate-bloom
**Co-occurs with**: Sweet, Citrusy, Berry, Tropical, Vanilla, Honey
**Dose behavior**: threshold extremely low (phenylethanol ~760 ppb); trace only in most applications; dominant in rose water/jasmine rice preparations — easily crosses into soapy at excess.

### 22. Floral-Bold
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Floral | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Indole, methyl anthranilate, gardenol — heavy, heady white-floral notes with a narcotic quality.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.75
**Anchor examples**: gardenia, tuberose, ylang-ylang, frangipani, night-blooming jasmine
**Tertiary descriptors**: heady-floral, narcotic-bloom, tuberose-like, gardenia-rich, indolic, creamy-floral, opulent-floral, honeyed-floral, full-bloom, seductive-floral
**Co-occurs with**: Floral-Delicate, Tropical, Sweet, Honey, Milk Chocolate
**Dose behavior**: trace is critical — crosses into overpowering/funky (indole becomes fecal) at low ppm; rarely dominant except in dessert applications.

### 23. Blossom-Citrus
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Floral | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Linalool, linalyl acetate, anthranilate methyl ester — the citrus-flower bridge that combines rose phenylethanol with lemon-like terpenes.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.75
**Anchor examples**: orange blossom water, neroli, lemon blossom, bergamot flower
**Tertiary descriptors**: orange-blossom, neroli, citrus-floral, petal-citrus, blossom-fresh, honeyed-orange-flower, bitter-blossom, perfumed-citrus, spring-bloom
**Co-occurs with**: Citrusy, Floral-Delicate, Honey, Vanilla, Sweet
**Dose behavior**: trace in baking (1 tsp orange flower water per loaf); dominant in French/Moroccan pastry — degrades rapidly with high heat.

### 24. Floral-Herbal
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Floral | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Linalool + camphor (lavender), bisabolol (chamomile), and sesquiterpene hydrocarbons — bridges floral and woody-herb.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.75
**Anchor examples**: culinary lavender, chamomile, rose geranium, hibiscus (partial)
**Tertiary descriptors**: lavender-like, chamomile-apple, tea-floral, herbal-flower, camphor-floral, geranium-leaf, tisane-like, aromatic-petal, soap-adjacent (at excess)
**Co-occurs with**: Cooling, Fresh Herb, Honey, Floral-Delicate, Citrusy
**Dose behavior**: trace only — above 0.5% lavender bud by mass in dough becomes soapy; dominant only in specialty shortbreads/syrups.

## AROMA — HERBAL/GREEN FAMILY (4 dimensions)

### 25. Fresh Herb
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Herbal/Green | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Cis-3-hexenal, linalool, eugenol (basil), aldehyde E2-hexenal (cilantro), with volatile oils concentrated in leaf glandular trichomes.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.8
**Anchor examples**: basil, cilantro, parsley, dill, chive, tarragon, mint (overlap)
**Tertiary descriptors**: fresh-herbaceous, leafy, basil-sweet, cilantro-bright, parsley-clean, dill-feathery, chive-oniony-green, tarragon-anise, just-picked, verdant, chopped-green
**Co-occurs with**: Fresh, Grassy, Vegetal, Citrusy, Floral-Herbal
**Dose behavior**: added at end of cook to preserve volatiles; trace as garnish; dominant as pesto or chimichurri base — oxidation browns color and shifts aroma to hay within hours.

### 26. Woody Herb
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Herbal/Green | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Thymol (thyme), carnosic acid + 1,8-cineole (rosemary), thujone (sage), carvacrol (oregano) — phenolic terpenes that survive long cooking.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: rosemary, thyme, sage, oregano, bay leaf, marjoram
**Tertiary descriptors**: rosemary-piney, thyme-earthy, sage-savory, oregano-Mediterranean, bay-laurel, marjoram-sweet, resinous-herb, hearth-herb, slow-cook-herb, fragrant-dried
**Co-occurs with**: Resinous, Warming, Cooked Meat, Bread-Crust, Savory Spice
**Dose behavior**: trace with 1 sprig per liter braise; moderate for infusion; dominant in herb-crusted roasts — stands up to hours of cooking unlike fresh herbs.

### 27. Grassy
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Herbal/Green | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Cis-3-hexenol, hexanal, and green-leaf volatiles (GLVs) from lipoxygenase cleavage of linoleic/linolenic acid in damaged leaves.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.8
**Anchor examples**: freshly cut grass, lemongrass (citral+GLV bridge), green tea (matcha), pea shoots, shiso
**Tertiary descriptors**: grassy, hay-fresh, cut-grass, matcha-green, green-tea, lemongrass-bright, pea-shoot, verdant-raw, meadow-like, chlorophyll-forward
**Co-occurs with**: Fresh, Vegetal, Melon, Citrusy, Fresh Herb
**Dose behavior**: present whenever chlorophyll-rich plants are cut; trace in salads; dominant in matcha, green-juice, or pea-forward dishes — oxidizes to "haylike" over days.

### 28. Vegetal
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Herbal/Green | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Pyrazines (2-isobutyl-3-methoxypyrazine in green bean/pepper), terpinene, and sulfur compounds (methional in asparagus) — raw-vegetable character.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.8
**Anchor examples**: green bean, asparagus, artichoke, celery, fennel stalk, bell pepper
**Tertiary descriptors**: vegetal, stalky, celery-bright, asparagus-earthy, artichoke-nutty, pepper-raw, bean-green, rooty-raw, underripe, garden-fresh, stemmy
**Co-occurs with**: Fresh Herb, Grassy, Mineral, Brassica (partial), Umami (asparagus)
**Dose behavior**: diminishes sharply with cooking (pyrazines volatile above 80°C); trace as raw garnish; dominant in raw preparations like crudités — negative descriptor in over-blanched vegetables.

## AROMA — WOODY FAMILY (3 dimensions)

### 29. Resinous
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Woody | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Alpha- and beta-pinene, limonene, juniperol — monoterpenes from coniferous oleoresins.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: pine needle, juniper berry, cedar tip, spruce tip, mastic
**Tertiary descriptors**: piney, resinous, conifer-like, juniper-sharp, cedar-dry, spruce-green, turpentine-adjacent, forest-like, terpenic, sappy, balsamic-pine
**Co-occurs with**: Cooling, Woody Herb, Bark-Wood, Barrel-Aged, Warming
**Dose behavior**: trace is balanced (e.g., 2 juniper berries in stock); dominant in gin-style infusions — above trace readily smells medicinal.

### 30. Barrel-Aged
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Woody | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Oak lactones (cis/trans whisky lactone), vanillin from lignin breakdown, eugenol and furfural from toast — extracted into liquids during extended wood contact.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: bourbon, aged rum, oak-aged wine, tawny port, sherry vinegar
**Tertiary descriptors**: oaky, barrel-note, whisky-lactone, vanillin-woody, toasted-oak, coconut-oak, bourbon-barrel, sherried, cask-aged, cooperage, char-wood
**Co-occurs with**: Vanilla, Warming, Warm Sweet Spice, Dried Fruit, Cocoa-Roast
**Dose behavior**: barrel extraction follows Fickian diffusion; trace at 3-month contact; dominant at 4+ years — char level and toast shift the dim toward Charred.

### 31. Bark-Wood
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Woody | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Methyl salicylate (birch/wintergreen), safrole (sassafras — now regulated), and guaiacol from soft-hardwood smolder.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.8
**Anchor examples**: birch syrup, sassafras root (in root beer), sarsaparilla, hickory bark
**Tertiary descriptors**: birch-syrupy, sassafras-like, root-beer, sarsaparilla, medicinal-bark, wintergreen-adjacent, forest-floor-wood, bark-sweet, tree-syrup-wood
**Co-occurs with**: Tree-Syrup, Toffee-Caramel, Warm Sweet Spice, Resinous, Molasses-Cane
**Dose behavior**: rare in modern cuisine; trace in artisanal syrups; dominant in traditional root beers — specific compounds like safrole are dose-regulated.

## AROMA — SPICY-AROMATIC FAMILY (4 dimensions)

### 32. Warm Sweet Spice
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Spicy-Aromatic | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Cinnamaldehyde (cinnamon), eugenol (clove, allspice), myristicin (nutmeg) — phenolic/aldehyde aromatics with warm-bready perception.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.95
**Anchor examples**: cinnamon bark, clove bud, nutmeg, allspice, star anise, mace
**Tertiary descriptors**: cinnamony, clove-warm, nutmeggy, allspice-rich, star-anise, mulled-spice, baking-spice, holiday-spice, mace-like, warm-aromatic, chai-spice
**Co-occurs with**: Sweet, Warming, Vanilla, Dried Fruit, Molasses-Dark, Honey
**Dose behavior**: trace ~0.1% mass; dominant at 1%+ in chai/mulled wine — heat amplifies release of eugenol and cinnamaldehyde.

### 33. Savory Spice
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Spicy-Aromatic | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Cuminaldehyde (cumin), linalool + geraniol (coriander seed), anethole (fennel), carvone (caraway) — earthy-aromatic aldehydes and ketones.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: cumin, coriander seed, fennel seed, caraway, nigella
**Tertiary descriptors**: cumin-warm, coriander-citrusy, fennel-anise, caraway-rye, nigella-onion-like, earthy-seed, toasted-seed-spice, curry-base, tagine-spice, Indian-spice
**Co-occurs with**: Exotic-Curry, Warming, Cooked Meat, Bean-Fermented, Vegetal

## AROMA — ROASTED FAMILY (5 dimensions)

### 39. Coffee-Roast
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Roasted | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Furaneol, 2-furfurylthiol, pyrazines, and sotolon develop through Maillard of Arabica/Robusta bean proteins and chlorogenic acid degradation during roasting.

**Dose behavior**: trace as rubbed on steak; moderate in tiramisu; dominant in espresso-based dishes — post-grind volatile half-life ~15 minutes.

### 40. Bread-Crust
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Roasted | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Pyrazines and acetyl-pyrrolines from Maillard reaction of flour proteins + starch with heat; ethyl propanoate esters from yeast development.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85

**Co-occurs with**: Bread-Yeast, Grain-Toast, Sourdough-Starter, Butter, Warming
**Dose behavior**: trace as crouton; moderate as crust element; dominant in bread-focused dishes — fresh baking aroma lasts 30-60 minutes before staling begins.

### 41. Meat-Roast
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Roasted | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Maillard products of amino acids + reducing sugars — 2-methyl-3-furanthiol (meaty), bis(2-methyl-3-furyl) disulfide, pyrazines from crust formation.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9

**Tertiary descriptors**: meat-roasted, seared-crust, Maillard-rich, browned-protein, roast-brown, rotisserie, grill-mark, crackling, caramel-meat, deep-roast-savory
**Co-occurs with**: Cooked Meat, Umami, Broth-Stock, Warming, Rich, Kokumi
**Dose behavior**: trace as seasoning crust; dominant in prime-rib crust; optimally forms at surface temps 140-160°C — higher produces burnt/acrid char.

### 42. Grain-Toast
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Roasted | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Maltol and isomaltol from starch pyrolysis; pyrazines from cereal protein Maillard; distinct from bread-crust by absence of yeast esters.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85

**Co-occurs with**: Sweet (malted), Butter, Bread-Yeast (overlap), Honey, Tree Nut
**Dose behavior**: trace when grains are nominally toasted; dominant in granolas and malted desserts — maltol at 100 ppm gives strong malty sweetness.

### 43. Cocoa-Roast
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Roasted | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Roasting cocoa bean triggers Maillard + Strecker cascades producing 2-methylbutanal, phenylacetaldehyde, and dimethyl sulfide — the "cocoa" note distinct from chocolate's milk/sweet profile.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: cacao nib, unsweetened cocoa powder, dark chocolate 85%+, cocoa tea, chocolate liquor

**Co-occurs with**: Dark Cocoa, Coffee-Roast, Bitter, Barrel-Aged, Warm Sweet Spice
**Dose behavior**: trace as dusting; moderate in mole/chili; dominant at 85%+ chocolate — distinct from sweet Milk Chocolate aroma.

## AROMA — CARAMELIZED FAMILY (4 dimensions)

### 44. Toffee-Caramel
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Caramelized | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Diacetyl, furaneol, and maltol formed through caramelization of sucrose at 160-180°C — soft-toffee note without burnt edges.

### 45. Burnt-Sugar
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Caramelized | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Further pyrolysis of caramelization products (185-200°C) yields furfural, hydroxyacetyl, and dark colorants — bitter-edged "dark caramel" / "burnt" note.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9

**Co-occurs with**: Bitter, Toffee-Caramel, Coffee-Roast, Molasses-Dark, Cocoa-Roast
**Dose behavior**: trace adds depth to dark sauces; dominant in crème brûlée crust; crosses to Charred above ~210°C.

### 46. Maple-Syrup
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Caramelized | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Fenugreek-lactone sotolon + caramelized-sap phenolics (vanillin, syringaldehyde) developed during evaporation of maple sap.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: Grade A maple syrup, maple sugar, maple butter, maple-smoked bacon
**Tertiary descriptors**: maple, maple-syrup, maple-sweet, maple-butter, sap-caramel, Vermont-sweet, birch-syrup-adjacent, sotolon-rich, autumn-sweet
**Co-occurs with**: Sweet, Warm Sweet Spice, Butter, Tree Nut, Cured-Preserved (maple bacon)
**Dose behavior**: trace 0.5-1 tbsp per 4 servings for background; dominant in glazes — Grade-B (now "Dark") has stronger sotolon than Grade-A Amber.

### 47. Molasses-Dark
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Caramelized | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Caramelization of beet/cane sugar refining byproducts concentrates diacetyl, maltol, sotolon, and melanoidin colorants; distinct from Molasses-Cane (uncaramelized).

## AROMA — SMOKY FAMILY (4 dimensions)

### 48. Hard-Wood-Smoke
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Smoky | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Guaiacol, syringol, and 4-methylguaiacol from lignin pyrolysis of dense hardwoods — intense, bold smoke with phenolic character.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0
**Anchor examples**: hickory-smoked brisket, mesquite-grilled steak, oak-smoked fish, black-walnut smoke

**Co-occurs with**: Meat-Roast, Cured-Preserved, Charred, Warm Sweet Spice (BBQ rub), Warming
**Dose behavior**: trace under 1 hour exposure; moderate 2-4 hours; dominant 8-12+ hours pit-smoke — mesquite is most aggressive and crosses to acrid fastest.

### 49. Soft-Wood-Smoke
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Smoky | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Similar phenolics as hard-wood but with higher 2-furaldehyde and softer lignin composition — fruitwood smoke reads sweeter and lighter.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: applewood-smoked pork, alder-smoked salmon, cherry-wood brisket, pear-wood sausage
**Tertiary descriptors**: applewood-smoke, alder-smoke, cherry-wood, fruit-wood, sweet-smoke, light-smoke, delicate-smoke, orchard-smoke, maple-smoke (overlap with Maple-Syrup)
**Co-occurs with**: Dried Fruit, Sweet, Meat-Roast, Cured-Preserved, Barrel-Aged
**Dose behavior**: trace yields hint of smoke; dominant in Pacific Northwest salmon traditions — gentler than hardwoods, less acrid buildup.

### 50. Charred
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Smoky | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Pyrolysis products when surface exceeds ~300°C — heterocyclic amines (HCAs), polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), and carbonized sugars.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0
**Anchor examples**: grilled eggplant skin, burnt scallion, blackened tortilla, char siu crust, blackened fish
**Tertiary descriptors**: charred, blackened, carbonized, burnt-edge, singed, flame-kissed, char-bitter, carbon-crust, grill-char, flame-licked, ash-edge
**Co-occurs with**: Bitter, Hard-Wood-Smoke, Meat-Roast, Bread-Crust, Warming
**Dose behavior**: trace via flash-char of peppers; moderate on grill crusts; dominant in whole-charred vegetable preparations (char siu, baba ghanoush).

### 51. Cold-Smoke
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Smoky | **Scale**: unipolar

**Dose behavior**: trace surface treatment; dominant in lox and Scandinavian traditions — shelf-life limited since no heat kill-step.

## AROMA — SULFUROUS FAMILY (3 dimensions)

### 52. Allium
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Sulfurous | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Alliinase cleaves S-alk(en)yl-cysteine sulfoxides into thiosulfinates (allicin, propanethial S-oxide) upon cell rupture — cooked forms shift to milder sulfides and mercaptans.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0 (raw) / 0.8 (cooked)
**Anchor examples**: garlic (raw/cooked), onion, leek, shallot, chive, scallion, ramp
**Tertiary descriptors**: garlicky, oniony, leek-sweet, shallot-refined, pungent-allium, sulfur-sweet (cooked), roasted-garlic, confit-garlic, green-onion, ramp-wild
**Co-occurs with**: Pungent (raw), Cooked Meat, Umami, Kokumi, Warming, Savory Spice
**Dose behavior**: raw bites hard — trace at 1-2 cloves per dish; cooked tolerance is much higher — dominant at 10+ cloves confit; browned allium skews toward Toffee-Caramel.

### 53. Brassica
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Sulfurous | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Myrosinase hydrolyzes glucosinolates into isothiocyanates (sulforaphane, allyl-ITC) and dimethyl sulfide — diagnostic of Brassicaceae.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: cabbage, cauliflower, broccoli, kale, Brussels sprouts, kohlrabi
**Tertiary descriptors**: brassica, cabbagey, cauliflower-sulfur, broccoli-green, kale-bitter, sprout-like, mustardy-vegetable, sulfury-green, over-cooked-cabbage (excess)
**Co-occurs with**: Pungent, Vegetal, Bitter, Mineral, Brassica
**Dose behavior**: trace in raw slaws; moderate in quick-cooked dishes; dominant in long-cooked forms — overcooking (>10 min) releases H2S and reads as stale.

### 54. Protein-Sulfur
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Sulfurous | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Hydrogen sulfide and mercaptans from cysteine/methionine thermal decomposition in eggs, meat, and legumes — distinct from onion/garlic S-alkyl profile.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: hard-boiled egg yolk, stewed pork, overcooked legumes, aged duck, braised kelp
**Tertiary descriptors**: sulfur-protein, egg-sulfur, yolk-deep, boiled-egg, mercaptan, protein-cooked-sulfur, stewed-meat-sulfur, rotten-egg (excess), legume-sulfur
**Co-occurs with**: Umami, Cooked Meat, Broth-Stock, Kokumi, Salty
**Dose behavior**: trace is desirable (complex savory); dominant reads as stale — aging eggs ≥3 weeks notably increases H2S formation upon boiling.

## AROMA — EARTHY FAMILY (4 dimensions)

**Co-occurs with**: Umami, Kokumi, Root-Tuber, Broth-Stock, Aged Cheese, Musty
**Dose behavior**: trace as garnish; moderate in risotto; dominant as mushroom duxelles — truffle oil typically contains synthetic 2,4-dithiapentane (considered inferior).

### 56. Root-Tuber
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Earthy | **Scale**: unipolar

### 57. Mineral
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Earthy | **Scale**: unipolar

### 58. Musty
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Earthy | **Scale**: unipolar

## AROMA — DAIRY FAMILY (5 dimensions)

### 59. Fresh Milk-Cream
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Dairy | **Scale**: unipolar

### 60. Butter

### 61. Cultured Dairy
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Dairy | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Lactic acid bacteria (Lactococcus, Streptococcus) generate lactic acid, acetaldehyde, and diacetyl; bifidobacteria add ethyl butyrate.

### 62. Aged Cheese
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Dairy | **Scale**: unipolar

**Co-occurs with**: Umami, Kokumi, Salty, Fatty, Nutty, Rich
**Dose behavior**: trace as garnish; moderate grated into dish; dominant as primary flavor — aging time correlates directly with intensity up to ~36 months.

### 63. Blue-Moldy Cheese
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Dairy | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Penicillium roqueforti/glaucum produces methylketones (2-heptanone, 2-nonanone), free fatty acids (caproic, caprylic, capric), and ammoniacal breakdown products.

**Dose behavior**: polarizing — trace crumble on salad works broadly; dominant in blue-cheese dressings or over walnut tart; high-methylketone loads read as ammonia to novice palates.

## AROMA — FERMENTED FAMILY (5 dimensions)

### 64. Lacto-Pickled
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fermented | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Lactobacillus and Leuconostoc metabolize sugars into lactic acid, ethanol, CO2, and esters; allyl-ITC and isothiocyanates remain in brassicas.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: kimchi, sauerkraut, kosher dill pickle, fermented hot sauce, tepache
**Tertiary descriptors**: lacto-pickled, sauerkraut-like, kimchi-funky, fermented-brassica, sour-pickle, brined-live, tangy-ferment, lacto-sour, probiotic-vegetable, dill-pickle-deep
**Co-occurs with**: Sour, Brassica, Pungent, Salty, Spicy (kimchi)
**Dose behavior**: trace as condiment; moderate as side; dominant in kimchi-jjigae — mature ferments (>4 weeks) develop more funk from yeasts joining lactobacilli.

### 65. Acetic-Vinegar
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fermented | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Acetobacter oxidizes ethanol to acetic acid; ethyl acetate and trace phenolics define varietal vinegars (balsamic, sherry, malt).
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: white vinegar, apple cider vinegar, sherry vinegar, balsamic, rice vinegar
**Tertiary descriptors**: vinegary, acetic, tangy-sharp, cider-vinegar, balsamic-sweet, sherry-vinegar-nutty, rice-vinegar-soft, malt-vinegar-toasty, sharp-acid, barrel-vinegar
**Co-occurs with**: Sour, Sweet (balsamic), Barrel-Aged, Fresh, Bright
**Dose behavior**: trace as final splash; moderate in vinaigrette (3:1 oil:vinegar); dominant in pickling brines — volatile acetic acid reads nasally sharp even at low dose.

### 66. Fish-Fermented
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fermented | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Salt-driven autolysis of fish proteins by endogenous cathepsins + halophilic bacteria produces glutamate-rich liquid (nuoc mam, garum), trimethylamine (TMA), and IMP nucleotides.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.95
**Anchor examples**: fish sauce (nuoc mam), colatura, anchovy paste, worcestershire, garum
**Tertiary descriptors**: fish-sauce, funky-fish, fermented-anchovy, garum, nam-pla, umami-fish, pungent-brine, seafood-fermented, glutamate-deep, funky-savory

### 67. Bean-Fermented
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Fermented | **Scale**: unipolar

### 68. Sourdough-Starter

### 74. Shellfish

### 75. Briny

## AROMA — MEATY FAMILY (4 dimensions)

### 76. Cooked Meat
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Meaty | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Maillard of protein + reducing sugars yields 2-methyl-3-furanthiol, bis(2-methyl-3-furyl) disulfide, pyrazines, and Strecker aldehydes — broad "meaty" character.

**Co-occurs with**: Meat-Roast, Umami, Broth-Stock, Kokumi, Rich, Warming
**Dose behavior**: trace in broths; moderate in stews; dominant in braised mains — long low cooking develops peptide-driven depth beyond surface Maillard.

### 77. Cured-Preserved
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Meaty | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Salt-driven water loss, enzymatic proteolysis, lipid oxidation controlled by nitrite/nitrate, and mold/yeast surface ripening produces isovaleric acid, octenone, and cured-meat methylketones.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9

**Co-occurs with**: Salty, Fatty, Umami, Cold-Smoke, Savory Spice, Rich
**Dose behavior**: trace as garnish; moderate integrated; dominant as wrap/primary protein — curing time strongly correlates with intensity (12+ months for premium hams).

### 78. Gamey
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Meaty | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Higher myoglobin + branched-chain fatty acids (4-methyl-octanoic acid in lamb, skatole in game birds) + aging increases compound concentration.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: venison, duck (with skin), lamb, rabbit, wild boar, pigeon
**Tertiary descriptors**: gamey, lambic (lamb), duck-rich, venison-earthy, wild-meat, bloody-meat, hanger-aged, skatolic, barnyard-meat, offal-adjacent
**Co-occurs with**: Cooked Meat, Barrel-Aged, Woody Herb, Berry, Rich, Bold
**Dose behavior**: perception varies across cultures; trace common in European cuisine, dominant in specific game preparations — dry-aging 21+ days intensifies.

### 79. Broth-Stock
**Category**: aroma-secondary | **Parent**: Meaty | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Long simmer extracts gelatin, free amino acids, IMP/GMP, and lipids; delicate pyrazines from bone roasting; glutamate-dominant palate.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: chicken stock, beef bone broth, dashi (overlap), pho broth, fond blanc

## TEXTURE — MECHANICAL (6 dimensions)

### 86. Crunchy
**Category**: texture-mechanical | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: High-frequency (2-10 kHz) acoustic emissions + high initial bite force; cell-wall rupture of rigid vegetable/grain tissue.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.1
**Anchor examples**: raw carrot, apple, celery, fresh bell pepper, granola, kale stem
**Tertiary descriptors**: crunchy, snappy, crisp-raw, loud-crunch, carrot-crunch, apple-bite, celery-crisp, brittle-crunch, audible-crunch, break-point, fresh-crunch
**Co-occurs with**: Firm, Dry, Fresh, Fibrous, Cool (often)
**Dose behavior**: requires <10% moisture loss to preserve; trace at garnish; dominant in raw-vegetable dishes — wilting above 24 hours postharvest drops crunch linearly.

### 87. Crispy
**Category**: texture-mechanical | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Lower-frequency (1-3 kHz) acoustic emission + thin brittle layer — characteristic of fried or baked surfaces with rapid Maillard and dehydration.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0
**Anchor examples**: fried chicken skin, tempura batter, potato chip, cracker, tortilla
**Tertiary descriptors**: crispy, crackly, chip-crisp, tempura-light, fried-crispy, cracker-snap, crust-crisp, light-crispy, shatter-crisp, golden-crisp
**Co-occurs with**: Dry, Fatty (fried), Bread-Crust, Meat-Roast, Warm
**Dose behavior**: requires surface <5% moisture; dominant in fried/baked surfaces; loss to sogginess accelerates above 60% RH environment.

### 88. Chewy
**Category**: texture-mechanical | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: High elasticity + protein/polymer network resistance to shear — requires repeated mastication cycles before bolus formation.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0
**Anchor examples**: caramel candy, chewy cookie, well-developed bread crumb, braised brisket bite, mochi
**Tertiary descriptors**: chewy, elastic, toothy, pulling-chew, gummy-chew, bread-chew, jerky-chew, caramel-chew, mochi-stretch, resilient-chew
**Co-occurs with**: Moist, Tender (balance), Starchy, Gummy, Fibrous (meat)
**Dose behavior**: gluten development, hydration, and gelation govern chew; trace with al dente pasta; dominant in jerky/caramel — mouth time increases perception.

### 89. Tender
**Category**: texture-mechanical | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Low force-to-deform + short mastication path; collagen conversion to gelatin (long braise) or young protein with minimal connective tissue (cuts like tenderloin).
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: slow-braised short rib, poached fish, baby greens, soft tofu, butter-poached scallop
**Tertiary descriptors**: tender, fork-tender, soft-tender, melting, fall-off, pillow-soft, tender-chew, delicate-break, silky-tender, melt-in-mouth
**Co-occurs with**: Moist, Juicy, Creamy, Delicate, Body
**Dose behavior**: inversely correlated with Firm; requires moisture + heat + time for tough cuts; trace as "youthful" in raw greens, dominant in braised roasts.

### 90. Firm
**Category**: texture-mechanical | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Rigid cellular architecture or dense protein matrix; requires moderate-to-high bite force; distinct from crunchy (firm can be quiet).
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0
**Anchor examples**: al dente pasta, firm tofu, medium-rare steak, halloumi, firm peach
**Tertiary descriptors**: firm, bite-resistant, dense-firm, al-dente, meaty-firm, tofu-firm, steak-firm, structural, solid-bite, cohesive-firm
**Co-occurs with**: Chewy, Crunchy (overlap), Dry, Body, Rich
**Dose behavior**: central to "perfect cook" heuristics; trace undesirable (mushy), dominant undesirable (tough); mid-range optimal for most cooked proteins.

### 91. Fibrous
**Category**: texture-mechanical | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Aligned muscle fibers (meat) or plant cellulose fibers (celery, rhubarb, asparagus stalk) that separate along grain with force.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0
**Anchor examples**: pulled pork, flank steak, celery stalk, rhubarb, asparagus base, jackfruit
**Tertiary descriptors**: fibrous, stringy, pulled-meat, shredded, stalk-like, stringy-celery, meat-grain, sinewy, fiber-separating, pull-apart
**Co-occurs with**: Chewy, Firm, Cooked Meat, Vegetal (plant fibers), Body
**Dose behavior**: trace in desirable pulled meat; dominant can read as tough/stringy — cooking against the grain reduces perceived fibrousness.

## TEXTURE — MOUTHFEEL (10 dimensions)

### 95. Creamy
**Category**: texture-mouthfeel | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Emulsion of fat droplets (<5 µm) in aqueous phase + surfactant-protein coat; smooth coating with no graininess.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: heavy cream, crème fraîche, emulsified butter sauce, custard, gelato
**Tertiary descriptors**: creamy, velvety-cream, smooth-creamy, emulsion-smooth, custardy, gelato-creamy, cream-sauce, whipped-creamy, dairy-smooth, panna-like
**Co-occurs with**: Silky, Rich, Fatty, Fresh Milk-Cream, Butter, Body
**Dose behavior**: emulsion stability is the key; trace at glazed sauces; dominant in creamed soups and gelatos — particle size above 10 µm reads grainy.

### 96. Silky
**Category**: texture-mouthfeel | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Low friction viscosity fluid + minimal suspended solids; Newtonian flow at palate temperature.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: consommé, clarified butter, olive oil, silky tofu, royal icing
**Tertiary descriptors**: silky, smooth-silky, flowing-smooth, satin-smooth, consommé-silk, clarified-silky, lush-silky, silken-tofu, satin-mouthfeel, pure-smooth
**Co-occurs with**: Creamy, Delicate, Body (consommé), Tender, Umami
**Dose behavior**: distinct from creamy (no fat emulsion requirement); dominant in clarified preparations — any suspended solids >20 µm break the silk.

### 97. Oily
**Category**: texture-mouthfeel | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Free lipid layer coats oral surfaces; CD36 tactile signaling + surface lubrication. Distinct from Fatty (taste) though co-occurs.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: chili oil, olive oil, sesame oil, duck fat, schmaltz-drizzled dish
**Tertiary descriptors**: oily, slick-oil, lipid-coated, greasy, oil-finish, chili-oil, olive-oil-slick, lubricating, lipid-mouth, after-oil
**Co-occurs with**: Fatty, Rich, Spicy (chili oil), Seed (sesame), Body
**Dose behavior**: thin layer (1-2 mL on plate) is finishing; dominant read as greasy (>10 mL per portion) — room-temp oils lubricate more than warm oils.

### 98. Grainy
**Category**: texture-mouthfeel | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Suspended solid particles 30-200 µm detected by palate/tongue papillae — cornmeal, polenta, undissolved sugar, egg curdle.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: polenta, cornmeal muffin, gritty custard (curdled), undissolved cocoa, pear skin
**Tertiary descriptors**: grainy, gritty, sandy-mouth, polenta-grain, cornmeal-grit, semolina-rough, curdled-grain, particle-mouth, undissolved, coarse
**Co-occurs with**: Dry, Starchy, Powdery, Crunchy (overlap at larger grit), Grain-Toast
**Dose behavior**: defect if unintended (curdled sauce); feature in polenta/grits; threshold of perception ~25 µm — sugar crystals above 50 µm feel crunchy instead.

### 99. Gummy
**Category**: texture-mouthfeel | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Long hydrocolloid polymer chains (xanthan, pectin, konjac) create elastic, sticky mouth resistance without emulsion smoothness.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: gummy candy, mochi, jelly, agar jelly, ovomaltine spread
**Tertiary descriptors**: gummy, rubbery, elastic-sticky, jelly-like, mochi-gum, pectin-gum, konjac-bounce, gelee, jellied, chewy-rubber
**Co-occurs with**: Chewy, Sticky, Gelatinous, Sweet (candy), Starchy
**Dose behavior**: above 0.3% xanthan produces noticeable gumminess; dominant in gummy candies — strongly disliked in hot mains.

### 100. Starchy
**Category**: texture-mouthfeel | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Gelatinized starch swells into semi-translucent paste; amylose retrogradation yields pasty coating distinct from silky or creamy.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: mashed potato, overcooked pasta, thick roux, congee, rice pudding
**Tertiary descriptors**: starchy, pasty, thick-starch, congee-smooth, mashed-potato, roux-coated, glossy-starch, gelatinized, potato-pasty, cornstarch-gel
**Co-occurs with**: Creamy, Body, Grainy (overlap), Butter, Umami (in risotto)
**Dose behavior**: starch ≥3% by mass yields perceptible thickening; dominant above 6% — pasting temperature and retrogradation govern long-term mouthfeel.

### 101. Gelatinous
**Category**: texture-mouthfeel | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Collagen-derived gelatin network (or agar, pectin) sets via hydrogen bonds; melts at body temperature producing distinctive palate-melt.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: bone broth aspic, pork trotter, panna cotta, gelee dessert, pho broth (cold)
**Tertiary descriptors**: gelatinous, aspic-like, jellied-broth, wobble, quiver, collagen-rich, melt-in-mouth-gel, panna-gel, gelee, gummy-gel
**Co-occurs with**: Body, Kokumi, Broth-Stock, Gummy, Creamy (panna cotta)
**Dose behavior**: gelatin at 0.5-1% sets to firm gel; palate melting below 35°C defines premium — agar or pectin alternatives do not melt and read differently.

### 102. Foamy
**Category**: texture-mouthfeel | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Gas bubbles (1 µm-5 mm) stabilized by surfactant proteins or hydrocolloids; light, aerated mouthfeel with rapid collapse.
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0
**Anchor examples**: cappuccino foam, meringue, espuma, mousse, whipped cream
**Tertiary descriptors**: foamy, airy, frothy, light-foam, espuma-light, mousse-foam, whipped-light, aerated, bubble-mouth, cloud-like
**Co-occurs with**: Delicate, Creamy (mousse), Sweet (meringue), Carbonated (overlap), Body (low)
**Dose behavior**: requires stable surfactant; trace on top; dominant in mousses/espumas — stability 30 sec to 30 min depending on structure.

### 103. Powdery
**Category**: texture-mouthfeel | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Fine particles <25 µm that coat oral surfaces and absorb moisture — confectioners sugar, cocoa powder, matcha dust.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: confectioners sugar, cocoa powder, matcha, dried pollen, freeze-dried fruit dust
**Tertiary descriptors**: powdery, dusty, matcha-dust, cocoa-dust, confectioners-dust, floury, chalky-powder, pollen, fine-particle, snow-like
**Co-occurs with**: Dry, Grainy (at larger particle), Coffee-Roast (coffee dust), Dark Cocoa, Delicate
**Dose behavior**: trace as garnish dust; dominant in maamoul/bonbon coatings — rapidly absorbs saliva and can read as drying at heavy dose.

### 104. Sticky
**Category**: texture-mouthfeel | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: High-viscosity residue adheres to palate and teeth — concentrated sugars, reduced sauces, glutinous rice starches.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: caramel sauce, honey drizzle, sticky rice, toffee, dulce de leche, rib glaze
**Tertiary descriptors**: sticky, tacky, adhesive-mouth, glue-like, honey-sticky, caramel-sticky, glaze-sticky, teeth-stick, gooey, clingy
**Co-occurs with**: Sweet, Gummy, Moist, Starchy, Toffee-Caramel, Honey
**Dose behavior**: water activity (aw) below 0.7 concentrates sugars into sticky regime; dominant in sticky rice, BBQ glaze, toffee — often deliberate in desserts.

## TEMPERATURE (1 bipolar dimension)

### 105. Temperature
**Category**: temperature | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: bipolar (-6 to +6)
**Mechanism**: Thermal receptors (TRPM8 cold, TRPV1/TRPV3 heat) respond to actual tissue temperature; perception modulated by context (e.g., cooling menthol biases cold perception).
**Stevens exponent**: 1.0
**Anchor examples**: -6: frozen custard; -4: ice-cream; -2: gazpacho; 0: room-temp cheese plate; +2: warm-tepid; +4: braised mains; +6: just-seared steak / molten fondue
**Tertiary descriptors**: frozen, icy, chilled, cold, cool, tepid, warm, hot, piping-hot, molten, scalding, room-temperature, just-above-body
**Co-occurs with**: Cooling (menthol-cold), Warming (ethanol-warm), Spicy (perceived hot), Carbonated (cold beverages)
**Dose behavior**: optimal eating temperatures vary by dish archetype — ice cream at -12°C serves best, seared steak at 55°C internal; thermal shock pairings (cold + hot) are an explicit design choice.

## CHARACTER (7 dimensions)

### 106. Rich
**Category**: character | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Integrated perception of fat, umami, and kokumi intensity; correlated with satiety-signal activation in hypothalamus. Distinct from Body (weight/viscosity) and Bold (assertiveness).
**Stevens exponent**: 0.9
**Anchor examples**: foie gras, short-rib braise, chocolate ganache, butter-poached lobster, cassoulet
**Tertiary descriptors**: rich, indulgent, luxurious, decadent, satiating, depth-rich, filling-rich, opulent, full-flavor, fat-rich
**Co-occurs with**: Fatty, Umami, Kokumi, Body, Creamy, Cooked Meat
**Dose behavior**: trace lift; moderate in mains; dominant in celebration dishes — fatigue signal rises fast, and portions should decrease as Rich approaches 5-6.

### 107. Complex
**Category**: character | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: High count of distinct perceptual dimensions within the dish; perception peaks when 4+ dims sit at ≥2 intensity and interact (olfactory-trigeminal-gustatory).
**Stevens exponent**: 0.8
**Anchor examples**: mole negro, aged bordeaux, 12-spice masala, pho, classic french bouillabaisse
**Tertiary descriptors**: complex, layered, multi-note, nuanced, shifting, evolving, crescendo-flavor, thick-with-flavor, rich-in-notes, symphonic
**Co-occurs with**: Aged, Rich, Bold, Warm Sweet Spice, Umami
**Dose behavior**: minimum 3-4 contributing dims required to register; dominant in classical preparations (mole, curry) — excessive complexity above ~8 dims reads muddled.

### 108. Fresh
**Category**: character | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Perceptual signaling of just-made, just-harvested ingredient — preserved volatile top notes (citral, cis-3-hexenal) and crisp textures.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: ceviche, sashimi, garden salad, fruit plate, caprese, spring peas
**Tertiary descriptors**: fresh, vibrant, lively, bright, just-made, just-harvested, garden-fresh, vibrant-raw, green-fresh, zingy-fresh
**Co-occurs with**: Sour, Citrusy, Grassy, Crunchy, Cooling, Juicy
**Dose behavior**: trace insufficient to define; dominant in seasonal Mediterranean/Japanese dishes — time from prep to plate governs Fresh above all.

### 109. Aged
**Category**: character | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Extended storage triggers enzymatic/microbial/Maillard development — loss of top notes, gain of complex base notes, increased umami and kokumi.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: 24-month parmigiano, 5-year balsamic, dry-aged beef, 10-year whisky, vintage port
**Tertiary descriptors**: aged, matured, developed, cave-ripened, cellar-aged, vintage, long-aged, time-deep, seasoned-age, mellowed, patina-flavor
**Co-occurs with**: Umami, Kokumi, Aged Cheese, Barrel-Aged, Rich, Complex
**Dose behavior**: perceived log-linearly with aging time; trace at weeks; dominant at years — proteolysis and oxidation products dominate profile.

### 110. Delicate
**Category**: character | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Low-intensity balanced dims; no single dim above 2; emphasis on volatile top notes and light textures.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.8
**Anchor examples**: chawanmushi, dashi, poached sole, white tea, mint tisane, pearl onion confit
**Tertiary descriptors**: delicate, subtle, gentle, refined, light-balanced, whisper-light, ethereal, clean-delicate, understated, elegant-soft
**Co-occurs with**: Silky, Tender, Fresh, Floral-Delicate, Fresh Milk-Cream, Foamy
**Dose behavior**: inverse to Bold — dominant Delicate means nothing exceeds 2; trace presence absorbed into stronger dims.

### 111. Bold
**Category**: character | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: At least one primary dim above 4 intensity, plus supporting dims — assertive, immediately perceptible profile.
**Stevens exponent**: 0.95
**Anchor examples**: vindaloo, Szechuan mapo tofu, blue cheese dressing, kimchi stew, Thai larb
**Tertiary descriptors**: bold, assertive, big-flavor, punchy, forward, loud-flavor, declarative, strong-stated, pronounced, impactful
**Co-occurs with**: Spicy, Umami, Salty, Cured-Preserved, Blue-Moldy Cheese, Fermented
**Dose behavior**: inverse to Delicate; dominant when any single dim hits 5-6; excessive Bold crosses into Rich fatigue — often paired with palate cleansers.

### 112. Body
**Category**: character | **Parent**: — | **Scale**: unipolar
**Mechanism**: Perceived viscous weight on palate — governed by protein, gelatin, starch, and fat content combined. Distinct from Rich (flavor intensity) and Creamy (emulsion).
**Stevens exponent**: 0.85
**Anchor examples**: demi-glace, bone-broth ramen, cassoulet, full-bodied red wine, reduced bolognese
**Tertiary descriptors**: body, heavy-body, weight-palate, full-bodied, viscous-body, backbone, structural-weight, substantial, heft, dense-mouthfeel
**Co-occurs with**: Rich, Gelatinous, Starchy, Broth-Stock, Umami, Kokumi
**Dose behavior**: trace in consommé; moderate in bisque; dominant in demi-glace — viscosity at 5-30 cP governs most culinary palate perception.
